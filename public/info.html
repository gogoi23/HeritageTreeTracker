FREQUENTLY ASKED QUESTIONS

How are trees added/recognized as heritage trees?

Trees are formally recognized as heritage trees by the City Council for their unique size, age, hsitorical or horticultural significance. Once accepted by Council, Heritage Trees are designated with a small plaque so they can be identified by the public and listed in the Heritage Tree database.

What is the oldest heritage tree?

The oldest heritage tree is the American elm, also known as the Ulmus americana, which was designated as a heritage tree in 1973.

Where can I access my account?

Users can access their accounts by clickin the login button on the left tab of their screen.

How do I get achievements?

Users gain achievements by visiting trees. The more trees the user visits, the more achievemnts gained.

How can I find heritage trees?

Users can find heritage trees by going to the map page, then hovering their mouse (if on pc) over the tree icons. The icons will provide a pop up with some information about that specific heritage tree.

How do I find specific heritage trees?

Users find specific heritage trees by using the filters feature on the top of the map page. Hover over the filters menu and select the filter that you want to sort the heritage trees by. Then click the Redraw button to the right of the filters menu to redraw the map based on that specific filter. To clear the selected filters, click the Clear Filters button on the bottom left of the map page.
